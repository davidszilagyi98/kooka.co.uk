What is Plastering and Drylining?

Plastering is a skilled technique used to create smooth, durable surfaces on walls and ceilings. It involves applying layers of plaster to the substrate, such as brickwork or plasterboard, and then smoothing it to achieve a flawless finish. Plastering not only enhances the aesthetic appeal of a space but also provides a protective and structurally sound surface ready for painting, wallpapering, or other decorative treatments.
Drylining, also known as drywalling or plasterboard fixing, involves attaching plasterboard to interior walls or ceilings. These plasterboard are typically made from gypsum plaster sandwiched between two layers of paper or fiberglass. Drylining offers benefits like creating a smooth and even surface for finishing, improving thermal and acoustic insulation, and providing fire resistance. It is a versatile method used in modern construction and renovation to create partitions, conceal wiring and pipes, and enhance the overall functionality and appearance of interior spaces.
