What is External Wall Insulation (EWI)?

External Wall Insulation (EWI) involves applying insulating material to the exterior of buildings to improve energy efficiency, reduce heat loss, and enhance comfort. This method can save up to 35% on heating costs and increase property value. Typically, 90mm thick expanded polystyrene (EPS) boards are used, with various render finishes available for a customised look.

Is External Wall Insulation a Smart Investment?

Investing in external wall insulation (EWI) is a smart choice for homeowners facing significant heat loss. It is believed up to 35% heat lost is through uninsulated walls, which results in colder rooms and higher heating costs. Many homeowners attempt to alleviate this issue by putting the heating up, leading to substantial energy bills.

Solid walls without cavities exacerbate the problem by causing condensation when warm air meets cold surfaces, eventually leading to mould and damp issues.

Our exterior solid wall insulation effectively addresses these challenges by creating a robust thermal barrier. By insulating your home's external walls, you not only ensure consistent comfort throughout the year but also achieve substantial savings on heating and cooling expenses.

This investment not only enhances your home's appearance and increases its resale value but also contributes to a greener environment by reducing overall energy consumption and carbon emissions. Opting for external wall insulation represents a proactive step towards enhancing your home's comfort, value, and environmental impact, providing a comprehensive solution to insulation challenges.

External wall insulation (EWI) involves adding a layer of insulating material to the exterior walls of a building to improve energy efficiency. This process reduces heat loss, enhances comfort, and can significantly lower heating costs.

Insulating material, usually EPS boards, is attached to the exterior walls and covered with a protective render. This creates a thermal barrier that prevents heat from escaping.

External wall insulation offers numerous benefits, including improved energy efficiency, reduced heating bills, enhanced home comfort, and increased property value. Insulating the exterior walls also protects your home from weather damage and reduces noise pollution.

External wall insulation improves energy efficiency by minimising heat loss through the walls. This helps maintain a consistent indoor temperature, reducing the need for heating and cooling systems. Consequently, you can lower your energy consumption and save on utility bills.

External wall insulation can last for several decades, often up to 25-30 years or more, depending on the quality of materials and installation. Regular maintenance and inspections can help ensure the insulation remains effective over its lifespan.

The cost of external wall insulation varies based on factors such as the size of the property, the type of insulation materials used, and the complexity of the installation. On average, homeowners can expect to invest between £8,000 and £25,000. However, the long-term energy savings and increased property value often offset the initial investment.
